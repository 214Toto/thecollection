Enzymes

State the use of enzymes in diagnosis and clinical intervention
✓ Used as drugs
✓ Measuring the levels of enzymes in plasma is integral to diagnosis
✓ glucose in blood or urine detection by using immobilized glucose oxidase
✓ AIDS detection involves use of enzyme dependent ELISA technique
All the enzymes are proteins except........ ?
✓ ribozymes
Define a substrate.
✓ The substance upon which an enzyme acts
An enzyme-catalysed reaction consists of ?
✓ substrate
✓ Product
✓ Enzyme
Biologically Speaking enzymes are Big Particles... True or False ?
✓ True, Their molecular (size) weight ranges from few thousands to millions.
What is activation energy ?
✓ The amount of energy needed to convert a substance from ground state to transition state
Enzymes are highly specific compared to other catalyst. An enzyme catalyzes only specific reaction. Some general types of enzyme specificity are ?
✓ Group Specificity
✓ Substrate Specificity
✓ Optical Specificity
✓ Absolute Group Specificity
✓ Reaction Specificity
What is Substrate Specificity and give examples
✓ It is a concept that describes how Enzymes are specific towards their substrates
✓ glucokinase catalyzes the transfer of phosphate from ATP to glucose
✓ Aspartate transminase catalyzes the transfer of amino group from aspartate
While giving examples, explain Reaction Specificity
✓ A given enzyme catalyzing only one specific reaction
✓ For example, lipases only hydrolyze lipids, urease hydrolyzes urea. They do not catalyze any other type of reaction
✓ amino acid oxidase catalyze oxidation of amino acid
Give examples of enzymes that exhibit Group Specificity.
✓ Proteases are specific for peptide groups,
✓ glycosidases are specific to glycosidic bonds.
WHen it comes to Absolute Group Specificity, What is the target of the following enzymes; Chymotrypsin, Trypsin, Carboxypeptidases, Thrombin
✓ Chymotrypsin => Aromatic AA
✓ Trypsin => Basic AA
✓ Carboxypeptidases => LAst AA in the peptide/protein
✓ Thrombin => Arg-Gly bonds
Explain the conept of Optical Specificity in enzymes.
✓ enzymes are able to recognise optical isomers of the substrate and exhibit specificity of substrate on which they act
✓ enzymes of amino acid metabolism act on L-amino acid but not D-amino acids
✓ carbohydrate metabolism act only on D-sugars but not on L-sugars
The name of an enzyme has two parts. Describe these two parts
✓ The first part indicates name of its substrate
✓ second part ending in ‘ase’ indicates the type of reaction it catalyzes
each enzyme has code (EC) number, Which is a four-digit number. WHat does each of the Four digits represent
✓ first digit indicates major class
✓ second digit indicates sub class
✓ third digit denotes sub sub class
✓ final digit indicates specific enzyme.
List the major classes of enzymes
✓ Oxidoreductases
✓ Lyasaes
✓ Ligases
✓ Hydrolases
✓ Transferases
✓ Isomerases
What type of reaction do oxidoreductase enzymes catalyze. Give an exmaple
✓ They catalyze oxidation and reduction reactions
✓ Alcohol Dehydrogenase or Glutamate Dehydrogenase
What type of reaction do Transferase enzymes catalyze. Give an exmaple
✓ They catalyze transfer of groups
✓ Hexokinase
What type of reaction do Hydrolase enzymes catalyze. Give an exmaple
✓ They catalyze hydrolysis of peptide, ester, glycosyl etc. bonds
✓ Trypsin
What type of reaction do Lyase enzymes catalyze. Give an exmaple
✓ They catalyze removal of groups from substrates by mechanisms other than hydrolysis forming double bonds
✓ Fumarase
What type of reaction do Lygase enzymes catalyze. Give an exmaple
✓ They catalyze linking together of two compounds. The linking is coupled to the breaking of phosphate from ATP.
✓ Glutamine Synthetase or Acetyl-CoA Carboxylase
What type of reaction do isomerase enzymes catalyze. Give an exmaple
✓ They catalyze interconversion of optical, functional and geometrical isomers
✓ Triose phosphate isomerase or Alanine Racemase
Describe an active Site
✓ A part of an enzyme where the substrate attaches
An enzyme active site has two parts. Name em ?
✓ Catalytic site => portion (part) of the enzyme that is responsible for catalysis
✓ Binding site => part of the enzyme that binds with substrate. It determines substrate specificity
WHat amino acids are found as part of the active site
✓ Usually serine, histidine, cysteine, aspartate or glutamate residues make up active site.
Aside serving as a place for attachment for a substrate, what other role does the active site have ?
✓ Enzymes are named according to the active site amino acid
✓ For example, trypsin is a serine protease and papain is cysteine protease.
List the Models of an Active Site.
✓ Lock and Key Model
✓ Induced Fit Model
Inspite of the Loc and Key Model being easy and effecient, what are some of its limitaions ?
✓ This model was unable to explain the possibility of rigid active site combining with the product to form substrate in reversible reaction
What are some of the factors that affect enzyme action ?
✓ Enzyme Concentration
✓ Hydrogen Ion Concentration
✓ Temperature
✓ Substrate Concentration
✓ Inhibitors and cofactors
