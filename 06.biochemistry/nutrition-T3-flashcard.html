Define BMR
✓ Energy expended by the body when the voluntary activity is at a minimum
Under what conditions is a persons' BMR best examined ?
✓ Subject should be awake
✓ at complete physical and mental rest
✓ 12-hour after the last meal, in the post-absorptive state to avoid the so-called specific dynamic action
✓ at an equable temperature (about 250C)
What is the Expenditure of energy in the absence of Physical activity?
✓ Functions such as pumping of blood by heart
✓ Conduction of nerve impulses
✓ Gastrointestinal motility
✓ Work of breathing
✓ and maintenance of cation differences across membrane, especially the sodium-potassium gradient
What are the units that are used to measure BMR
✓ kcal/kg body weight/h for human beings
Comment on the normal values of BMR, in various tyes of individuals
✓ Higher in children than in adults
✓ Little higher in youn ger adults than in the elderly
✓ Little higher in males than in females
✓ depending on surface area it is calculated as kcal/sqm/h
What instrument is used to measure the BMR. Describe how it is used.
✓ Benedict-Roth apparatus
↣ Subject asked to breathe into the mouth piece of the apparatus
↣ the volume of oxygen consumed over a 6-minute period is determined
↣ This is multiplied by 10 to obtain the oxygen consumption in one hour

Comment on the BMR of a human and a mouse regerding there size and weight........
✓ BMR in humans is about 32 kcal/kg/day, whereas in a mouse it is 212 kcal/kg/day
Explain the variation in the BMR of people according to their weights
✓ , BMR varies with body weight, but the rise is not proportional. For instance, in males with body weight 60 kg, 70 kg, and 80 kg, the BMR is 2590, 3010, and 3710 kcal/day, respectively
between a person with lean body mass with a certain weight and another man of the same weight with an obese state... who has a higher BMR.... justify your answer
✓ adipose tissue is not as metabolically active as lean body mass
✓ , BMR is higher in the lean individual.
With regards to BMR what is it variation amoongst different individuals ?
✓ Significantly higher in infant and growing child than in adult
✓ a little higher in young adult than in the elderly.
So how does sex affect the BMR ?
✓ BMR varies between males and females, with males having a BMR that is typically about 5% higher than females
✓ This difference arises because males usually have a higher proportion of lean body mass (muscle) compared to females of the same weight.
How about Genetic differences affect the BMR ?
✓ The BMR may vary by up to 10% between subjects of the same sex, age, body weight and fat content.
In what way can the Nutritional state of an individual affect he/her BMI ?
✓ In starvation, there is an adaptive decrease in the BMR
✓ The converse is true when energy intake is increased.
How do hormones affect the BMR ?
✓ BMR is increased in hyperthyroidism as thyroid hormones have a stimulatory effect on metabolism
✓ In hypothyroidism, BMR may decrease by up to 40%, leading to weight gain
✓ Other hormones like epinephrine and G.H, tend to raise BMR
What relation does climate have with BMR
✓ BMR increases in cold climate. In the persons living in tropical climate, BMR is about 10% less than those living in temperate zones.
What drugs can stimulate or inhibit an increase in BMI
✓ Stimulate : Nicotine and caffeine & theophylline
✓ Inhibit : Beta Blockers
Definely Specific Dynamic Action (SDA) and also state its other names

✓ Diet-induced thermogenesis or post-prandial thermogenesis.

✓ Fats ⤍ 11-13%

Guess the SDA is a wasteful expenditure from viewpoint of cellular energetics. Surpport this staemembrt
✓ For instance, assume a person taking 250 g of protein, which should produce 250 x 4 = 1000 kcal. But the body must spend some energy (about 20% = 200 kcal) to extract this 1000 kcal. So the net value of 250 g protein is 800 kcal (1000 minus 200).
