RESPIRATORY SYSTEM DEVELOPMENT

Illustrate the functional division of the of the respiratory system
✓ Conducting Zone => entire upper respiratory tract & lower respiratory part up to terminal bronchioles
✓ Respiratory Zone => respiratory bronchioles, alveolar ducts & alveoli
Illustrate the anatomical division of the of the respiratory system
✓ Upper Respiratory System => External nose, nasal cavities, paranasal air sinuses, pharynx
✓ Lower Respiratory System => arynx, trachea, bronchi, terminal bronchioles, respiratory bronchioles & alveoli
The Primordia of the respiratory system develops from what ?
✓ a median endodermal diverticulum of the foregut (respiratory diverticulum)
✓ Into the splanchnopleuric intraembryonic mesoderm
What is the origin of the Lining epithelium of Respiratory System ?
✓ endoderm
What is the origin of the Cartilage, muscles & connective tissue components ?
✓ splanchnopleuric mesoderm surrounding the foregut
In what week does the laryngotracheal diverticulum develop ?
✓ 4th week
Which side of the Pharyngeal part of the foregut does the this diverticulum arise from ?
✓ The Ventral wall/side or The on the Floor of the Pharynx
What are some of the inducing factors that initiate development of this Primordial Respiratory diverticulum, and where do they come from ?
✓ Retinoic acid, and TBX4 transcription factor
✓ From surrounding mesoderm
as the Diverticulum extands caudally into the thorax it becomes bifid to form ?
✓ right & left bronchial/ lung buds
What structure seperates the ventral Pharyngeal wall & the proximal part of respiratory Diverticulum ?
✓ laryngotracheal groove
the laryngotracheal groove is bounded by which Pharyngeal arches
✓ 6th pharyngeal arch on either side
Describe he formation of the Laryngotracheal septum
✓ caudal part of respiratory diverticulum forms 2 lateral folds => tracheoesophageal folds
✓ They grow medially from the margins of the groove
✓ And they eventually fuse to form tracheoesophageal septum
This tracheoesophageal septum lies in what plane, and extends in what direction ?
✓ tracheoesophageal septum lies in the coronal plane & extends caudocranially
What is the function of this septum in its caudal Portion and in its Cranial Portion
✓ Cranial Portion => it doesn't close off completely but keeps leaves an opening into the primitive Pharynx called the larngeal orifice / Inlet of larynx / furcula of His
✓ Caudal Portion => separates trachea ventrally from the esophagus dorsally
the Lung buds invaginates into structures that will later come to form the right & left pleural cavities. What are these structures ?
✓ right & left pericardioperitoneal canals
The furcula of His is surrounded by...... ?
✓ mesenchyme of 4th & 6th pharyngeal arches derived from neural crest cells
When the mesenchyme surrounding the Inlet of orifice proliferates to form what structures ?
✓ Laryngeal cartilages
Aside forming the named structures above, what effect does proliferation of the mesenchyme have on the orientation of the laryngeal Inlet ?
✓ slit-like laryngeal inlet becomes U-shaped
✓ proliferation of mesenchyme of 4th & 6th arches makes the U-shaped laryngeal inlet into a T-shaped orifice
How does the laryngeal inlet attain its adult shape ?
✓ Reorganization of cartilages results in characteristic adult shape of laryngeal inlet
