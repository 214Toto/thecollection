Blood Composition, Propeties, functions and Volume

What Percent of blood is of cells ?
✓ 40- 45 %
What is the approximate number of RBCs per mm3 of blood. What is the name given to the condition when this number increases and when the number reduces ?
✓ Normally there are about 5 million RBCs per mm3
✓ When they are decreased ⇒ anemia
✓ > when they are increased ⇒ polycythemia
What is the approximate number of WBCs per mm3 of blood. What is the name given to the condition when this number increases and when the number reduces ?
✓ Normally, there are 4000 – 11,000 per mm3
✓ When they are decreased ⇒ leukopenia
✓ when they are increased ⇒ leukocytosis.
What is the approximate number of platelets per mm3 of blood. What is the name given to the condition when this number increases and when the number reduces ?
✓ Normally, there are 300000 per mm3
✓ When they are decreased ⇒ thrombocytopenia
✓ when they are increased ⇒ thrombocytosis
The plasma constitutes 55-60 % of the total blood volume, and it consists of water (90 %) and dissolved solutes (10 %). what are components that contain the latter ?
✓ Organic substances : Plasma proteins (7.1 %), lipids, hormones, enzymes, nutrients and waste products (2 % ). ✓(b) Inorganic substances (0.9 %) which include the various electrolytes e.g. Na+, K+, Cl-, HCO3-, Ca2+ and PO4 3

Define THE HEMATOCRIT (H) and state its formular
✓ Hematocrit (packed cell volume) of a sample is the ratio of the volume of erythrocytes to that of the whole blood.
✓ expressed as a percentage or preferably, as a decimal fraction
Differentiate Venous Hematocrit from Body Hematocrit
✓ Venous Hematocrit measures the proportion of red blood cell to plasma in the peripheral blood, but not in the entrie circulation.
✓ Body Hematocrit is the ratio of the total erythrocyte mass to the total blood volume
Describe how the procedures taken to determine the Hematocrit (H)
✓A blood sample is placed in a tube called Wintrobe tube that contains an anticoagulant and is then centrifuged for 30 mins at 3000 to 3500 rpm
✓ The erythrocytes will be packed at the bottom of the tube and above them is a layer of leukocytes, platelets and nucleated erythrocytes called the buffy layer (coat).
✓ Above the buffy coat is relatively cell-free plasma
From observing the hematocrit how would you be able to know the following; (1) Normal blood of a Male and that of a female. (2) A Person with Anemia (3) A Person with Polycythemia
✓ a normal male will have 42% - 52% Hematocrit
✓ a normal female will have 37% - 47% Hematocrit
✓ Anemia » a Depressed Hematocrit %
✓ Polycythemia » An elevated Hematocrit %
What is the clinical importance of Hematocrit determination
✓ Can be used to measure Renal Blood Flow
✓ is a good simple test for anemia or Polycythemia
✓ can be used to estimate the blood volume
✓ Gives a rough estimate of the size of erythrocytes and the concentration of erythrocytes.
express, in decimal form, the normal values of H of Males, Female and new borns
✓ Men 0.42 – 0.48
✓ Women 0.37 – 0.42
✓ Newborn 0.44 – 0.6
Explain, with examples, the factors that affect H
✓ Its value in small blood vessels is less than its value in large vessels due to plasma skimming
✓ It is ⇈ in polycythemia and cases of hemoconcentration
✓ It is ⇊ in anemia and cases of hemodilution
✓ greater in venous blood than in arterial blood due to chloride shift.
State the normal Values of the blood relative to a persons body wieght and body surface area.
✓ The blood volume is normally about 8 % of the body weight or 80 mL/Kg of body weight
✓ 3.2 litre/m2 of body surface area.
from the above stated normal values, calculate the total blood volume of a 70kg young adult male.
✓ 5.6 litres
Comment, with proper reasons, on the normal values of blood in females and ib obese persons.
✓ The normal values are less in females (and also in obese persons) due to their greater body fat content (which is relatively avascular)
What can cause an increase in the blood volume ?
✓ Pathological causes ⇉ polycythemia vera
✓ Physiological causes ⇉ pregnancy, high altitudes and following muscular exercise
What can cause a decrease in the blood volume ?
✓ Pathological causes ⇉ acute hemorrhage or shock
✓ Physiological causes ⇉ Menstruation or Postpartum Period of pregnancy
What components of blood are considered when calculating the blood volume ?
✓ The blood volume approximately equals the sum of the plasma volume and red cell volume (since the volume or other blood cells is negligible)
What principle can be used to measure either of the two components, the plasma volume or the red cell volume ?
✓ Dilution Principle => a method where a known amount of a substance (tracer) is injected into the bloodstream, and the concentration of this substance is measured after it mixes with the blood.
Since the total blood volume os the sum of the the plasma volume and the red cell volume is it then necessary to measure both for you to have the total blood volume ? Give a reason for your answer.
✓ No, it isn't necessary !
✓ The blood volume can be estimated if only one of them is measured and the hematocrit (H) is known
what methds can be used to determine the plasma volume ?
✓ Dye Method
✓ Isotope Method
Explain how the Dye method is carried out.
✓ A known amount of a dye e.g. Evans blue is i.v. injected
✓ after 5-10 min, the concentration of the dye in the plasma is determined
✓ the plasma volume can be determined, and the total blood volume can also be calculated if (H) is known
A person with a (H) of 40% was injected with 30mg of Evans Blue, and its plasma concentration was found to be 0.01mg/mL. Calculate the Total Blood volume of this person.
✓ 5000 ml
Why is Evans Blue preferred during this procedure ?
✓ It is non-toxic & not metabolized in the body
✓ Its plasma level is easily measured by spectrophotometer
✓ It isn't adsorbed to the RBCs and does not enter or hemolyze them
✓ It combines with the plasma proteins so does not rapidly escape into the tissue spaces & is also not rapidly excreted by the kidneys.
Explain the procedures of the Isotpoe method.
✓ A sample of serum albumin is incubated with I-131 (= radioactive iodine) in vitro
✓ An amount of this labeled albumin with known radioactivity is i.v. injected into the subject, and after l0-15 min, a blood sample is withdrawn, and the plasma radioactivity is measured
✓ The plasma volume can be determined, and the total blood volume can also be calculated if the (H) is known
Normal RBC volume regulation is acheived by nearly equal rates of their formation and destruction. In case of sudden blood loss, How does the body restore the volume.
✓ A rapid compensatory mechanism: Red cells are rapidly shifted from the blood stores (e.g. the spleen) into the general circulation.
✓ A slow compensatory mechanism: The rate of erythropoiesis in the bone marrow is increased as a result of 02 lack
The regulation of the plasma volume also involves two mechanisms, namely ?
✓ Water Balance: a slow mechanism that keeps a constant volume of plasma by either water loss of water gain
✓ Fluid Exchange: a rapid but limited mechanism that occurs at the level of the capillaries between the plasma and the tissue
What are the values of the Hydrostatic pressure and the Oncotic pressure at the arteriole ends and the venous ends of the capillaries.
✓ The hydrostatic pressure is 30-40 (average 32) mmHg at the arterial ends and 10-15 (average 12) mmHg at their venous ends
✓ oncotic pressure averages 25 mmHg allover
Why are fliuids filtered at the arteriole ends and reabsorbed at the venous ends ?
✓ Fluids are filtered at the arterial ends since the hydrostatic pressure exceeds the oncotic pressure there
✓ reabsorbed at the venous ends where the oncotic pressure exceeds the hydrostatic pressure.
Between the two process, filtration and reabsorption, which one is normally higher?
✓ Normally, the rate of fluid filtration slightly exceeds the rate of fluid reabsorption, and the excess fluid is drained back to the bloodstream by the lymphatics (lymph vessles)

What is the site of formation of the plasma proteins in the embryonic stage ?
✓ the mesenchymal cells
Among the Plasma proteins what is the order of formation.
✓ The first protein to be synthesized is albumin, followed by globulin and the other plasma proteins
What is the site of formation of the plasma proteins in an adult ?
✓ -the reticuloendothelial cells of the liver, bone marrow, degenerating blood cells, general body tissue cells, and the spleen also contribute to the formation of plasma proteins.
Where are Gamma Globulins formed ?
✓ from B lymphocytes, which in turn form immunoglobulins.
what are the sources of Plasma Proteins ?
✓ Food proteins
✓ Tissue Proteins
Discuss food protein as a source of Plasma Proteins
✓ most effective for synthesis of plasma proteins when: (l) Their structure is similar to that of the plasma proteins (2) They are of a high biological value (i.e. rich in the essential amino acids)
✓ Certain proteins favour albumin formation (e.g. those from muscle) while others favour globulin formation (e.g. plant proteins).
Tissue proteins exist in two forms, describe them.
✓ fixed tissue proteins that cannot be converted into plasma Proteins
✓ reserved tissue proteins which can be converted into plasma proteins
Reserved Plasma Proteins are of 2 types, List Them ?
✓ Dispensable reserve proteins
✓ Labile reserve proteins
Describe Dispensable Reserve Proteins
✓ These are mobilized from the tissues to the liver during starvation where they are used for energy production and plasma protein formation.
Describe Labile Reserve Proteins
✓ These are structurally similar to the plasma proteins. They are mobilized from the tissues to the bloodstream directly if the amount of the plasma proteins is suddenly decreased
