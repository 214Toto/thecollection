WHat is the ratio between RBcs and WBCs ?
✓ 1000:1
WHat is the avearge life span of RBCs ?
✓ 120 days
Write down the average values of the following of RBCs; (1) diamter (2) thickness (3)Volume (4) Normal count in males and in females
✓ diameter is 7.5 microns
✓ thickness 2 microns
✓ volume 87 cubic microns
✓ normal count averages 5.4 million in adult males
✓ normal count averages 4.8 million in adult females
Define Polycythemia and its types.
✓ Polycythemia is the ⇈ in the number of RBCs
✓ Primary Polycythemia => condition in which there is ⇈ production of R.B.C.s due to an unknown cause.
✓ Secondary Polycythemia => occurs whenever the body suffers O2 lack (which stimulates red cell production in the bone marrow).
What is the result of Primary polycythemia on an individuals blood circulation
✓ leading to increased hematocrit, blood volume and viscosity
✓ Increased viscosity will increase peripheral resistance which will increase BP
✓ The condition also leads to stagnant hypoxia
Explain how the cardiac output of a person suffering from primary polycythemia usually remains normal
✓ The increased blood volume tends to increase the venous return while the increased viscosity tends to decrease it. Hence having a normal cardiac output
Which peaople are likely to have secondary Polycythemia
✓ individuals living at high altitudes (in whom the red cell count may reach up to 6-8 million/mm3)
✓ newly born infants (due to the relative O2 lack during intrauterine life) in whom the hematocrit is about 60 %.
Why is it that an infant can survive 4-6 months without any form of iron based diet and still present no signs of Iron defeciency anemia ?
✓ Infants, who have a hig hematocrit, degreade the excess RBcs and the hemolyzed hemoglobin is utilized as a source of iron
✓ Another source of iron are various stores of the body
✓ a combination of these two can provide iron for erythropoiesis for 4-6 months
The cytoplasm of an RBC is mainly composed of Hb. Approximately how much of it is in the cytoplasm ?
✓ 30 pg of Hb
enlist others substances that you can find in the cytoplasm of a RBC
✓ electrolytes (specially K+ and HCO3)
✓ several enzymes e.g. carbonic anhydrase and glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase (G6PD)
Outline the layers of the RBC together with what they are formed from.
✓ An outer layer formed of glycoprotein
✓ A middle layer formed of phospholipids
✓ An inner layer formed of mucopolysaccharide
The biconcave shape of the RBC is produced by what proteins ?
✓ ankyrin
✓ spectrin
What condition can a person suffer from if the above mentioned proteins were to be defeced ?
✓ spherocytosis
........ is the major consituent of the cytoskeleton of the RBC ?
✓ Spectrin, a dimer consisting of α and β sub-units
What other coponents makes up the filaments found in the membrane cytoskeleton ?
✓ actin
WHat is the function of the integral proteins of the RBC and give examples
✓ They tightly bound to the membrane and act as anchors for the underlying membrane skeleton
✓ One has binding sites for several glycolytic enzymes
✓ They transport HCO- and Cl- leaving or entering the red cell.
✓ Examples; protein (Band) 3 and glycophorin A
What layer of the RBC membrane is responsible for determining blood group and why ?
✓ The outer layer, the one made of glycoproteins
✓ This glycoprotein layer contains the specific agglutinogens that determine the blood group
