Define Stool
✓ Waste residue of indigestible material from the GIT
What is the composition of stool
✓ ¾ water and ¼ undigested and unabsorbed material
✓ Epithelial cells, leukocytes
✓ Bacterial and inorganic material
✓ Bile salts and pigments
✓ Intestinal secretions- mucous
Outline the aim of stool examination
✓ Detect pathogenic organisms in the stool
The type of specimen use in this examination are ?
✓Stool (fresh random)
✓ Rectal swab
Who will collect the specimen ?
✓ Stool ⇒ The patient
✓ Rectal Swab ⇒ If stool is unobtainable, nursing staff or physician will collect rectal swab
What cautions must you take as you collect Stool ?
✓ Stool should be collected in a dry, sterile wide mouthed container
✓ The container should be uncontaminated with urine or any other body fluid
✓ Sample should be properly labelled and fresh sample should be tested
What of the rectal swab, what is its procedure of Collection ?
✓ Rectal swab should only be collected when it is not possible to collect stool
✓ Swab should be inserted in the rectum for 10s
✓ Care should be taken to avoid unnecessary contamination of specimen with bacteria from anal skin
Explain the criteria of sample rejection
✓ Specimen contaminated with urine, residual soap, or disinfectants
✓ Specimens received in leaking transport containers
✓ Dry specimens
✓ Specimens submitted in fixative or additives
What is the preferred quantity of the specimen ?
✓ at least 5g of faeces
