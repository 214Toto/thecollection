Comment on the absorption of Direct acting acetylcholine receptor agonists & CNS Penetration
✓ poorly absorbed from the gut and are not distributed to the CNS
Which receptors do Acetylcholine, bethanechol and Carbachol activate ?
✓ Acetylcholine and Carbachol activate both muscarinic and nicotinic receptors while bethanechol only activates muscarinic receptors
What are the ocular effects of activation of the muscarinic receptors ?
✓ Increase lacrimal gland secretion
✓ Contraction of the iris sphincter muscle leads to Miosis
✓ Contraction of ciliary muscle enables accommodation of the lens to focus on close objects
What are the respiratory effects of activation of the muscarinic receptors ?
✓ increase bronchial muscle contraction
✓ increases secretion of mucus in the entire respiratory tract
Due to their Bronchoconstriction effect, what conditions are they contraindicated ?
✓ asthma and other obstructive lung disease
What are the Cardiac effects of activation of the muscarinic receptors ?
✓ decrease impulse formation by reducing the rate of diastolic depolarization leading to slowed heart rate
✓ they slow conduction of the cardiac action potential through the AV node and thus leading to an increased PR interval
What are the Vascular effects of activation of the muscarinic receptors ?
✓ Acetylcholine typically causes vasodilation though vasoconstriction may occur under some conditions
Explain the Mechanism by which they cause vasodilation
✓ mediated by M3 receptors
✓ activation of the nitric oxide synthetase leading to formation of nitric oxide
✓ NO diffuses into vascular smooth muscle cells and activate guanylyl cyclase to ↑ the formation of cyclic guosine monophosphate which causes vasodilation
